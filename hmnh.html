The Online Shop at Harvard Museum of Natural History

Home/Portfolio/The Online Shop at HMNH

Overview:

E-commerce project for General Assembly UXDI

Role:

Research, Competitive Analysis, Sketching, Storyboard, User Flow, Site Map, Wireframes, Prototype, & Testing

Timeframe:

7 days

Tools:

Pen & DotPad, Sketch, InVision

The Challenge

Conduct a user research study at a museum to uncover customer and business needs that relate to shopping at the museum’s gift shop. Choose 1 problem to solve and design an online shopping experience that addresses that problem.

The Solution

An e-commerce site for the Harvard Museum of Natural History (HMNH) that allows users to browse products by exhibit and purchase items relevant to and memorable from their experience at the museum.

Research

User research was conducted with two other UXDI designers. We visited the HMNH gift shop twice for a period of 2-3 hours to conduct stakeholder & user interviews, as well as contextual inquiry. From the affinity diagrams, each designer developed their own problem statement and completed the remainder of the project individually.

Stakeholder interview: Wendy, Retail Supervisor

"I select products that go by our mission-- educational, revolve around animals, minerals, flora, fauna, and delight the senses."

The shop should be a seamless part of the experience in visiting a museum

Wendy hand picks every item that's sold. A lot of other museum gifts shops are run by outside companies that select all of the inventory and hire the staff

HMNH does not currently have an online gift shop, but does take phone orders

User Research

"Whatever you have in the shop should reflect what’s in the museum."

Many users were from out of town, some (local and non local) were return visitors, several were visiting HMNH for the first time; all had a strong appreciation for museums

Users value unique items, items that match what they saw in the exhibit, giftable products, and the ability to buy something after they visit

Shopping at museum shops--in person and online--is a great way to get ideas for and buy gifts

Storyboard

Jeff is a museum enthusiast and visits them wherever he travels. He recently visited the Harvard Museum of Natural History and loved the lifelike and informative exhibits.

Storyboard

When he returned from his trip, he realized he bought souvenirs for friends, but nothing for himself. Luckily, HMNH had a new online gift shop. He scrolled through the many exhibit related items, & easily found something memorable from his visit.
