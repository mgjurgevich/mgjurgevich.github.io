The gas is in the tank

April 8, 2013 · by Marisa · in Uncategorized

After experiencing the joy of completing a 21 mile run two weeks ago, subsequent miles have been slightly underwhelming, and, physically and mentally, training has been a bit of a roller coaster. Throughout the entire training season, I’ve heard that once you reach 20 miles, you can go 26.

When I reached the top of Heartbreak Hill from Hopkinton just over two weeks ago, I was beyond thrilled, as well as relieved, confident that in another 3 weeks I’d be ready to complete the next 5 miles. Yet, as the memory of that day fades further away and the reality of the marathon comes closer, it’s been hard to keep creeping feelings of doubt and uncertainty at bay.

It was no surprise that just about every inch of my legs were sore or that I felt all around fatigued for a few days after 21. But my mind started to wander when I was winded after running a couple miles one day, my achilles suddenly swelled in pain shortly after that, and a severe head cold left me completely exhausted, and from running, for days.

Although I’ve been trying not to speculate, it’s difficult not to rehash the training miles I’ve missed, the lousy 8 miles I ran on Saturday, or just how heavy my legs felt in Newton a few weeks ago and what that’ll all mean on Monday. At this point, it’s easy to get caught up in every single detail of the past 15 weeks and the next 7 days, but it’s not wise.

So what I keep reminding myself instead is that the gas is in the tank; I’ve logged the time and miles, what’s done is done. The only certainties are that there’s a marathon on Monday and that I’ll be at the starting line at 10:40. And the unknowns, when or how I get to the finish line, won’t happen or go well unless I believe I can make it there.
