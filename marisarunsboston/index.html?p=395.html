Granola bar recipe

March 18, 2013 · by Marisa · in Nutrition

After writing about making my amended Alton Brown granola bars last week, I figured I should include my own (nearly perfected) recipe. I’ll definitely be making and eating a batch of these before my 21 mile run on Saturday!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups oats
  • 2 cups mixed nuts (+ an optional dash of hemp or flax seeds)
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2-4 tbsp butter
  • 1/3 cup almond or peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp almond extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup dried fruit


Method

  1. Preheat oven to 350. Butter 9×9-inch pan.
  2. Combine chopped nuts with oats, spread onto a cookie sheet and toast in oven for 15 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes or so.
  3. Mix honey, brown sugar, butters, extract and salt in a saucepan over low-medium heat and cook until brown sugar has dissolved.
  4. When the oats/nuts have toasted, remove and decrease the oven temperature to 300 degrees.
  5. Combine oats/nuts with dried fruit and then add to the wet mixture, stirring until all dry ingredients are evenly coated. (I usually leave the heat on very low during this part and add extra butters and/or honey if neccesary.)
  6. Spread mixture into buttered pan, pressing on it with a spatula to even it out. Bake in the oven for 20-25 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven and allow bar to almost completelyÂ cool.
  8. Cut into squares in the pan, or if you can get it out, remove the whole bar and cut it into squares on a cutting board.
  9. Once the squares are completely cool, store them in an airtight container in the pantry or fridge for a week or so. If some (or all) of your bars crumbled, save that to top yogurt or ice cream, or to eat by the handful.
