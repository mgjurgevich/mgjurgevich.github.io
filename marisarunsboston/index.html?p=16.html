Colder days ahead

November 23, 2012 · by Marisa · in Weather

Yesterday I ran 3.5 miles, which counted as my first day of training, and as RunKeeper pointed out, the furthest distance I’ve run in a long time. During and after my run, I wasn’t really thinking about how much further I need to go. I was thinking about how cold it’s going to get.

On Thursday, it was fifty degrees and sunny: near perfect running conditions. Over the next five days, the daily highs rarely go much over forty. Needless to say I’m not encouraged by the fact that Winter is still technically a month away.

Yesterday, running with the sun shining brightly, leaves still on some of the trees and more crunching beneath my feet, I felt really happy to be outside, taking in such a beautiful day. It made running the furthest I have in a while easy and enjoyable. Imagining doing the same with snow, ice, and slush in place of late fall’s discarded foliage is hard.

I can’t deny that I’m uneasy about getting out there for two and three times that distance in the depths of winter. But I am hopeful that the most difficult of my initial efforts in getting back into shape will happen over the next month, when I’ll be able to enjoy at least a handful of pretty-warm-for-November/December days.
