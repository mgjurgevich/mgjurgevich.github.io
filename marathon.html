Boston Marathon Charity Team Finder

Home/Portfolio/Marathon

The Boston Marathon is one of the most prestigious, and arguably the most challenging, marathons in the world.

It's also really difficult and competitive to qualify for. The Boston Athletic Association (B.A.A.) provides a limited number of entries for local non-profits to create charity teams. This allows 6,000 amateur runners to enter the race and 200+ nonprofits to raise millions of dollars.

Overview:

Open topic project for General Assembly UXDI

Role:

Research, Competitive Analysis, Sketching, Wireframes & Prototype, Visual Design

Timeframe:

7 days

Tools:

Pen & DotPad, Sketch, InVision

The Current Landscape

There is no single platform to match runners that didn't qualify for the Boston Marathon with teams and causes that provide an alternate opportunity to participate in the race.

The Solution

The Boston Marathon Charity Team Finder expands the visibility of charity teams, and makes it easier for runners to find a cause they’re interested in, and fulfill a big goal for themselves and the charity.

Research

Conducted user interviews to determine:

  • What would motivate people to run on a charity team
  • Learn about their memorable experiences participating in any other races
  • Hone in on what information they would look for when finding a team.

Interviewed nonprofit and charity team managers to:

  • Learn about their experience finding team members
  • Identify what they would improve with the current process

Competitive Analysis:

  • Existing sites that list charity teams: Boston Athletic Association, John Hancock Non-Profit Program, Charityteams.org
  • Nonprofits with the largest Boston Marathon teams: Dana Farber Cancer Institute
  • Nonprofits that seek out volunteers for other races/event: Multiple Myeloma Research Foundation Your Own Race Program
  • E-commerce sites for best practices and inspiration on information architecture, navigation, filtering, sorting, and searching: Zappos, ModCloth, B&H Photo Video

Primary User: Amateur Runner

About

"More races should unite cause with community in this way."

  • Occasional runner/athlete
  • Busy work and social schedule
  • Most memorable racing experiences are with friends, family, colleagues

Goals

"Running my first half marathon was very hard but finishing was the best feeling ever."

  • Benefit the community
  • Feel a sense of accomplishment
  • Have fun

Needs

"Finding a cause I believe in would make me more motivated to take on such a big challenge."

  • Motivation beyond the race itself
  • Accountability
  • Clear expectations

Pains

"Running was hard, but other than that it was great."

  • Finding the right opportunity
  • Understanding requirements and where to apply
  • Pressure to meet targets

Secondary User: Non-profit organization

The secondary user is the manager or staff person at a non profit that has been accepted into the charity team program.

There are two ways that non-profits can apply for and be granted bibs for a charity team:

  • The BAA Official Charity Program, which offers bibs to 25-30 charities

  • The John Hancock Non-profit Marathon Program, which provides ~180 non-profits with marathon bibs.

Key Takeaways:

Getting the opportunity to form a charity team is a big deal, and can lead to a lot of new fundraising dollars and donors.

Recruiting and supporting a team can also be a challenge, especially for those without the infrastructure to manage the project.

There are a lot of B.A.A./official rules, which can be confusing to potential runners and team managers.

"I share the same information over and over again, all throughout the year. It would be great to have the rules and requirements in one place." - Carol, Tenacity Volunteer & Team Manager
