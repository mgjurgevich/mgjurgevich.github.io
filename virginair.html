Virgin America: Reimagining the flight booking experience

Home/Portfolio/Virgin America

Timeframe:

5 days

Tools:

Pen & DotPad, Sketch

The Challenge

Improve the ticket purchasing flow for purchasing round trip tickets from one US city to another for one adult leaving on any date and returning three days later, up to and including selecting seats.

The Solution

A ticket selection experience on Virgin America that allows users to set search preferences so that they can save time and feel better about their purchase and travel decisions.

Usability Testing

I started by observing three users book a 3-day, domestic round trip ticket for one adult on Virgin America. I asked them to think out loud and share how they were feeling throughout the process.

The Good

"As far as airlines go, this is above par."

Primary action is front & center, not a lot of superfluous information

Actions are clearly indicated/validated

Single page loads quickly

Avatars lighten mood

Forms are clean and easy to fill out

Guiding information throughout site (On time percentages, seat viewer, etc.)

The Bad

"They didn’t tell me I’d have to fly through San Francisco! They should be up front and not try to trick you."

The calendar is oversized, low contrast, and “a little too responsive”

Total travel and layover times “felt hidden”

Not clear why some seats cost more; no ability to downgrade cabin

All testers had to restart the booking process from the beginning at least once

Experience started positively, ended negatively

Analysis & Iterations

Virgin America screenshot:

Departure/Return date calendar picker

Virgin America's current site makes it easy to select dates for return and departure dates, providing the user just a date and flight price.

Virgin America screenshot:

Flight selection page

Once dates are selected, users are given additional flight, price, and seat options to review. Flights with layovers are referred to as 'connecting flights'.

Sketches:

Flight selection decisions

I sketched the calendar and flight picker pages, comparing the number of apparent vs. actual decisions. Selecting a flight appears simple on the calendar page, but users are presented with an additional number of options to consider on the following flight selection page.

Sketches:

Flight selection decisions

I started thinking about ways to view more information on the calendar page, as well as a hierarchy for the decisions on the selection page based on user research and the booking information that was most important to their buying decision.

Calendar view:

Iteration 1

I wanted users to have options on this view so that flights more relevant to their flying prefences would be shown on the selection page (as it exists in the current user flow). I also increased the calendar's color contrast.

Calendar view:

Iteration 2

On the second iteration, I added additional filtering options as secondary navigation under select departure date. But, adding the same 4 options to the calendar made that view too crowded. User feedback on this screen also indicated that filter wasn't the correct term to use.
