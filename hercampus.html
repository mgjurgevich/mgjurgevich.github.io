Her Campus

Home/Portfolio/Her Campus

Her Campus is a Boston­ based media company and online magazine for female college students and recent graduates, ages 17­-24.

Our job was to provide recommendations for a comprehensive, mobile-first website redesign.

Overview:

3 designer project for General Assembly UXDI

Role:

User Research, Business Analysis, Sketching, User Flows, Mobile wireframes & prototype, Usability Testing

Timeframe:

13 days

Tools:

Pen & Graph Paper, Google Analytics, Sketch, InVision

Objective

Declutter and streamline the website’s page layouts, particularly on landing pages.

Focus on a mobile first design.

The Solution

A data­ driven approach to promoting and highlighting content on both mobile and desktop.

Research

We began our research with Her Campus stakeholders to understand the business priorities and needs. We started the user research process by conducting usability tests with 5 women, ages 20-25, to gather initial insights on the existing Her Campus site.

Stakeholder interview

It’s important to keep readers on the page.

There are many ideas about what might work, such as changing ad placements, making “Recommended Articles” more appealing, or “prioritizing and organizing information.”

Ads are an essential part of the business.

The Her Campus look should be simple but unique and innovative.

Usability Testing

Users liked that the sections weren’t grouped by multiple categories. They could find what they were looking for without a lot of guessing.

Ad placement was confusing; users didn't realize they were on the home screen and didn't elect to read some content because they assumed it was an ad.

Feelings about the content were mixed; users questioned why they saw the same articles repeated in sections (Trending, Buzzworthy) as they scrolled.

User Interviews

We then interviewed 9 women, ages 19 to 23, to understand this demographic’s online reading habits and find out what keeps them engaged. We also surveyed 15 college age women to gather general information about their lifestyle website choices.

Key Takeaways

Buzzfeed is incredibly popular because the content is always fresh, and the quizzes and videos are fun. Users like the site for its entertainment value.

"I love videos. I can stay on a site for hours without noticing even." -Danielle, 21

The women we spoke to are task oriented internet users. They go to a site for a specific reason, but won’t stay on it if they don’t see something else they’re interested in.

"I won't pay attention to the recommendations if the article I'm reading isn't good." -Rachel, 21

Screen Inventory & Analytics

After interviews, we took an inventory of all the screens in the existing Her Campus desktop and mobiles sites and documented their primary purpose. We also evaluated Google Analytics to better understand which pages were most read and where there is opportunity for more engagement.

Screen Inventory: Key Takeaways

Visual hierarchy not clear, everything is given equal prominence

Wide variety of layouts on each page

Too much separation of related content: comments, social media, suggested and related content are too far from article

Several different recommendation lists on each page; repetition between lists

Google Analytics

Nearly 70% of users are mobile

75% of users find Her Campus through organic search or social media--mainly Facebook & Pinterest, the rest are direct referral

Social users that land on an article page have the highest bounce rate

The most viewed category pages are "Love" and "Entertainment"

Guiding Principles:

Our primary user is a college­ age reader of lifestyle magazines, who is looking to skim through content that interests her so that she can be entertained.

By simplifying the layout and highlighting content that readers prefer (such as videos and quizzes), they will be more interested in browsing and engaging with the site.

Prototyping, Testing, & Iterating

Research insights narrowed our scope to a mobile first design and indicated the importance of the article page. We started with a design studio for the article page, built a paper protoype with pieces of each designers sketches, and began testing.

Testing the paper prototype provided key insights for designing wireframes: sections/categories at the end of the article were not important, a recommendation list of related content within the article was well received, & a read more button with an in-scroll ad was easily navigated.

With user feedback and the goal of simplifying layouts across all pages, wireframes for the article, category, & home pages were created in Sketch.

Wireframes: Article Page

Testing the read more/ad placement in digital form was a top priority. Users easily navigated the read more button, but felt that when they resumed reading, the ad was too close to the recommended articles.
Wireframes: Article Page

Users liked having in scroll images and a comments section closer to the end of the article.
Wireframes Article Page

Users didn't continue scrolling past 2-3 recommendations and wanted to see articles from outside the category.

Usability testing feedback was incorporated into the article, category, and home designs in the final iterations. The article page and was used as the main guide for implementating changes that would better engage the reader. From there, a pattern for sections and articles was incorporated into the layouts for the home and category pages.
